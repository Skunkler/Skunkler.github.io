About Stop Link 101


What is "stop link101"?

If you are looking us up for the first time, thank you for checking us out! We are a group of individuals affected by the link101 corridor project in district 43 in South Eastern Indiana.

The link101 project (put forth by Governor Holcomb, and senator Randy Frye via INDOT) is a 2 lane highwway that runs through our area. This project is advertised as an improvement in our area for: safety, economic, and convenience purposes. On INDOT's website, this project looks essential, and wanted. This is untrue.

Many hundreds of people have been to the meetings that INDOT has been holding since last spring, and almost every person there is against this. It is an overwhelming majority. This highway will destroy our rural economy in favor of a suburban economy. There are farms and homesteads that have been in people''s families since the 1800s that could be destroyed along these proposed routes. The people in Dearborn, Ripley, Ohio, and Switzerland counties are not just opposed to this project, they are devastated.

If you look at INDOT questionaires regarding this project, they ask you to choose your preferred route. Nowhere do they list "no build" as an option.

They leave you to fill that in under "other". Not only that, but in their data reflected from these meetings, they are supposed to tabulate people who choose "no build". If you didn't fill out the form specifically the way they want, and only left a negative commend, your input wasn't tabulated in the data. Instead, it is put at the back of a 400 page report, in the "comments sections".

This is by design. The real data isn't being shown. The people of the area aren't being represented. This is entirely un-American, and unacceptable. We are a group dedicated to exposing the duplicitous nature of what is being proposed in our small towns. Ripping up homes, homesteads and family farms for a secrete private stakeholder that has yet to be identified, is a misuse of eminent domain. We will not stand for this misuse of government power. The key steakholders should be the public, and we have spoken. They will not run this project through our town. Not now, not ever. Thank you for your support!
